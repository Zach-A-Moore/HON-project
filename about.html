About EcoTrack

A food & water footprint tracker built as an honors research project at Minnesota State University Moorhead (MSUM).

📝 Start Logging
🌊 What Is a Water Footprint?

A water footprint measures the total volume of freshwater used — directly and indirectly — to produce the goods and services we consume. It was introduced by Prof. Arjen Hoekstra in 2002 and is now a widely used metric for understanding water sustainability.

💧
Blue Water
Surface and groundwater actually consumed — irrigation water drawn from rivers, lakes, or aquifers that is incorporated into the product or evaporated.
🌱
Green Water
Rainwater stored in soil that is used by plants. Green water makes up the majority of most crops' footprint and comes "for free" from rainfall.
🏭
Grey Water
Fresh water required to dilute pollutants to safe levels. This represents the water pollution load from agriculture and industry.
🥩
Animal Products
Meat has one of the highest water footprints because animals consume large quantities of feed crops — each requiring water to grow — before becoming food.
🌍 Why It Matters

Freshwater is one of Earth's most precious and limited resources. While 71% of our planet is covered in water, only about 2.5% is freshwater, and less than 1% of that is easily accessible. Yet global water demand continues to rise due to population growth, changing diets, and climate change.

📊
Agriculture = 70% of Freshwater
About 70% of the world's freshwater withdrawals go toward agriculture — making food choices one of the biggest levers we have to reduce water use.
🐄
The Beef Comparison
Producing 1 kg of beef requires ~15,400 L of water. The same weight of vegetables requires ~322 L — nearly 48 times less water.
🚰
Water Scarcity
Over 2 billion people live in countries experiencing high water stress. By 2025, 2/3 of the world could face water shortages (UN Water).
🌡️
Climate Connection
Climate change is intensifying droughts and altering rainfall patterns globally, making sustainable water use more urgent than ever.
⚙️ How EcoTrack Works

EcoTrack combines three types of water tracking into a single daily log:

💧
1. Drinking Water
Track how much water you personally drink each day. The WHO recommends at least 2–2.5 L/day for adults, though needs vary with body size, climate, and activity level.
🍽️
2. Food Water Footprint
Each food item has a pre-computed water footprint in liters per serving, sourced from the Water Footprint Network database. When you log meals, EcoTrack sums the total "virtual water" embodied in your diet for the day.
🏠
3. Household Water Use
Track direct water use at home — showers, toilet flushes, laundry, dishwasher, and running faucets. EcoTrack applies industry standard flow rates to convert your inputs into liters automatically.

📱 How to Use EcoTrack:

  1. Click Log Entry each day and record your drinking water, meals, and home water use.
  2. Visit the Dashboard to see real-time charts of your footprint trends.
  3. Check History to review past entries, filter by date, and export data.
  4. Use Goals to set daily targets and get personalized recommendations based on your habits.
  5. All data is stored locally in your browser — no account needed, fully private.

🎓 About This Project

EcoTrack was developed as part of an Honors Program project at Minnesota State University Moorhead (MSUM). The goal of the project is to raise awareness about how everyday food and consumer choices translate into real freshwater demand — both the visible water we use at home and the invisible "virtual water" embedded in our food.

The application is entirely client-side: all processing happens in your browser and data is stored only in localStorage on your own device. No data is ever transmitted to a server.

Built with plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Charts powered by Chart.js. Hosted on GitHub Pages.
